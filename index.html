Gap Analysis
Zero Genuine Activity Despite 69,560 EnrolledCritical
99.79% of members have never filed a claim or submitted a transaction
All members were bulk-enrolled in May 2026. No activation message — SMS, push, or WhatsApp — was sent post-enrollment. The only transactions in the system are 3 internal test entries. Members do not know they are enrolled or what action to take.
Points Expiry Imminent — Claims Backlog at RiskCritical
All issued and frozen points expire June 30 2026 — weeks away
906,102 points in PENDING claims and 601,593 in REJECTED claims face an imminent expiry. If the claims backlog is not cleared before the expiry date, points will be forfeited — damaging retailer trust at the very start of the program. Expiry must be extended immediately.
OCR Quality Too Poor for Auto-Approval at ScaleCritical
89.5% of invoice scans fall below the auto-approval confidence threshold
Claims below the threshold land in the CS manual review queue. With 521 of 582 claims requiring manual review, the team cannot operate at scale. Root causes: low-resolution photos, handwritten invoices, poor lighting conditions. A pre-submission quality gate in the app would eliminate most low-quality submissions before they enter the queue.
KYC Completion Near-Zero Across 69,560 MembersMajor
Only 7 of 69,560 members have completed identity verification
Identity verification is a prerequisite for point redemption. While drug license verification is complete for all members, the deeper Aadhaar-based KYC has essentially not been activated. This blocks any redemption once the claims backlog is resolved and genuine points start flowing.
All Claims Show the Same Product Code Regardless of InvoiceMajor
Every one of the 582 claims uses the same product identifier
The earning rule covers 278 eligible products in the SKU master. However, all claims show the same product code — suggesting the app is not capturing the actual product from the OCR output, or the matching logic is using a placeholder. This needs investigation to confirm whether earning is being applied correctly.
No Tier or Volume DifferentiationModerate
All 69,560 members earn at the same flat rate with no progression incentive
High-volume retailers and occasional purchasers are treated identically. No tiers, multipliers, or milestone rewards exist. Once the baseline program is operational, tiered earning for top-purchasing retailers would significantly increase per-member value and loyalty.
Prioritized Actionables
1
Extend points expiry — protect retailer trust at program launch
All current and pending points expire at the end of June 2026. If the claims backlog is not cleared in time, members will see their points vanish at their very first interaction with the program. Extend the expiry window to at least December 2026. This is a configuration change with no build cost but high trust impact.
Platform Config · Owner: Loyalife program admin
2
Add a photo quality gate before claim submission
89.5% of invoice photos are below confidence threshold. Add real-time feedback in the app before submission: blur detection, lighting check, and required field visibility confirmation. Show a "Your image may be rejected — try retaking" warning. This stops poor-quality photos from entering the CS queue before they are submitted.
App UX · Configuration + minor build · Owner: Loyalife product + Lupin ops
3
Send activation campaign — 69,553 members have never engaged
A simple SMS to enrolled retailers: "You're registered on Lupin Samruddhi rewards. Scan your next Lupin invoice to start earning." Include the eligible medicines list. The BE (Business Executive) and AM (Area Manager) field hierarchy is already mapped — BEs can personally follow up with their assigned retailers. Even 1% activation = ~695 new participating members.
Communications · No build required · Owner: Lupin field team + Loyalife comms config
4
Clear the 217 PENDING claims backlog — assign a dedicated CS sprint
217 claims are stuck in the CS review queue with no communication sent to the members who filed them. Assign a focused review to action all 217 — prioritising claims with confidence scores above 50%. Clear the backlog before the expiry extension goes live so members can see the result of their submissions.
CS Operations · Manual review · Owner: Lupin CS team
5
Investigate the single-product-code issue and verify claim matching
All 582 claims show the same product code regardless of what was scanned. Determine whether this is a UI capture bug (OCR output not being read) or a rule matching issue. Confirm that claim evaluation is running against the full 278-product eligible list and not a placeholder. Retroactive correction may be needed for the 3 approved claims.
Platform Investigation · Owner: Loyalife engineering + Lupin program manager

Gap Analysis
48.6% Redemption Rate — Half of Earned Points UnredeemedCritical
83.9M points sitting in member accounts; redemption declining
The program has issued 163M points but members have redeemed only 79.1M. A significant portion of unredeemed points belong to members who have transacted but never explored the redemption catalogue. No reminder communications are configured. Without prompting, breakage rises: unredeemed points represent real programme liability and signal weak perceived catalogue value.
26.2% of Activated Members Have Never Made a BookingMajor
609 activated members with zero booking transactions
These consultants completed account activation but have not converted to program participants. No automated follow-up sequence exists after activation. A booking incentive for first-timers — even a small bonus — combined with a single direct communication could convert a meaningful portion of this cohort.
Point Expiry Policy Creates Aggressive ForfeituresMajor
Points expire on a rolling 12-month basis from issue date
The tier alert system flags members approaching expiry, but no automated notification is sent to warn them. Members who take a seasonal break from booking (e.g., off-peak periods) silently lose accumulated points. An email trigger at the 60-day and 30-day mark before expiry would reduce forfeitures and improve member satisfaction.
Top-Heavy Booking Distribution — Bottom 40% UnderservedMajor
Top 10% of consultants = 62% of booking value
The flat 1€ = 1 point structure provides no additional incentive for mid-tier or occasional consultants to increase their booking activity. No milestone bonuses, seasonal multipliers, or achievement badges exist. A tiered structure (e.g., Platinum consultants earn 1.5× during peak months) would increase activity from the middle of the distribution without impacting top performers.
No Notifications Configured Across the Entire ProgramModerate
0 automated emails or push notifications have ever been sent
The Loyalife notification framework supports email, push, and SMS triggers based on events (booking made, points expiring, milestone reached). Worldia has not activated any of them. Members receive no acknowledgment when they earn points, no alert when their balance is at risk, and no nudge when they are inactive. Notifications are the single highest-leverage action available at zero build cost.
Japan Market Overexposure — Single Market = 32% of VolumeModerate
Japan represents an estimated 32% of total booking value
Heavy concentration in a single market creates fragility — a geopolitical event, pandemic-related restriction, or shift in consumer preference could significantly affect program revenue. No secondary market has more than 12% share. Diversification incentives (bonus points for bookings to underperforming markets) would reduce concentration risk and grow overall volume.
Prioritized Actionables
1
Configure point expiry notifications — 60-day and 30-day triggers
Points expire on a rolling 12-month basis. No warning is currently sent to members before expiry. Configure two automated email triggers: one at 60 days before expiry and one at 30 days. The message should show the member's expiring balance and link directly to the redemption catalogue. This is a configuration task requiring no development work.
Notifications Platform config Owner: Worldia program admin + Loyalife CSM
2
Launch re-engagement campaign for 609 zero-booking activated members
609 consultants have activated their account but made no bookings. Send a single personalised email: "You have [X] points waiting — make your first booking to start earning more." Include top destinations by booking value. Even a 15% conversion = ~91 new active members with measurable booking impact.
Communications No build required Owner: Worldia marketing + Loyalife comms
3
Send redemption reminder to the top 500 members by unredeemed balance
83.9M points sit unredeemed. Identify the 500 members with the highest unredeemed balance and send a targeted email with their current points value, expiry date, and 3 featured catalogue items. High-balance members with a near-expiry window will convert at the highest rate. Tie it to a seasonal theme for better open rates.
Redemption No build required Owner: Worldia CRM team
4
Introduce a mid-tier multiplier for consultants booking 2× or more destinations
The current flat earning structure treats €10,000 in Japan bookings identically to €10,000 across five destinations. Introduce a 1.25× multiplier for consultants who book more than 2 distinct markets in a rolling 30 days. This nudges the mid-tier (currently earning but not growing) without cannibalising top-performer economics.
Rule Config Earning design Owner: Worldia commercial team + Loyalife admin
5
Review catalogue relevance — check whether top redemptions match member demographics
48.6% redemption among 73.8% active members suggests catalogue relevance issues for a portion of the base. Pull the top 20 redeemed items and compare against the booking profile of non-redeeming members. Travel agency consultants may prefer travel-related rewards (hotel nights, lounge access) over generic merchandise. Even a small catalogue refresh targeted at the non-redeeming cohort would improve rates.
Catalogue Review Owner: Worldia product + Loyalife catalogue team

Gap Analysis
No Notification System ConfiguredMajor
0 automated communications have been set up since launch
No SMS, email, or push notifications are configured on the Alkem Rewards+ program. Members receive no confirmation after a claim is submitted, no update when a claim is approved or rejected, and no alert when points are credited. A two-level approval flow without any status communication leaves members uncertain about whether their actions have been registered.
Two-Level Approval Introduces Processing LatencyMajor
Claims require sequential sign-off from Area Sales Manager and Head Office
Each claim passes through two manual approval stages. Without a time-bound SLA for each approver, claims can stall at either level. Members submitting claims in bulk (end-of-month invoice uploads) may experience multi-week delays before points appear in their account — reducing the motivational link between the sales action and the reward.
No Data Available for Performance MonitoringMajor
Program data has not been exported since go-live in April 2026
No transaction data, engagement figures, or claim volumes are currently available for analysis. Without a periodic data export cadence (weekly or monthly), program performance is invisible to the account team. Configuration decisions cannot be validated, and no early signals of adoption or friction are accessible.
Flat Structure Treats All Chemists IdenticallyModerate
No tiers, multipliers, or milestone bonuses differentiate high-volume retailers
High-volume pharmacies and occasional purchasers earn at the same rate. No progression mechanic — tiered multipliers, seasonal bonuses, or milestone rewards — exists to incentivise chemists to increase stocking over time. Once baseline metrics are available, a tier structure designed around invoice frequency and volume would strengthen long-term retention.
Field Merchandiser Earning Relies on Manual VerificationModerate
Visibility activities are manually reviewed — no photo evidence capture configured
Shelf display and promotional material placement are valid earning activities, but verification depends on field managers reviewing and approving without a structured evidence trail. Requiring photo capture at the point of claim submission (shelf photo, branded display photo) would reduce fraudulent claims and give the compliance team a verifiable audit trail.
No KYC or Identity Verification Process DescribedModerate
Redemption prerequisites for chemist KYC compliance are unclear
Pharmaceutical retail loyalty programs in India often require distributor license or KYC verification before point redemption. It is not confirmed whether identity verification is configured as a pre-redemption gate or whether the current member base has completed any form of verification. This should be confirmed and communicated clearly to members before the first redemption cycle.
Prioritized Actionables
1
Configure claim status notifications for all approval stages
Members submitting claims receive no feedback at any stage of the approval process. Configure notifications for: (1) claim received confirmation, (2) ASM approval / rejection with reason, (3) Head Office sign-off and points credited. SMS is the recommended channel given the chemist retail audience. This is a platform configuration task that significantly improves member trust and program participation.
Notifications Platform config Owner: Alkem program admin + Loyalife CSM
2
Set approval SLAs for both levels — ASM (48h) and Head Office (72h)
The two-level approval chain has no enforced time limit. Claims can sit at either stage indefinitely. Define and enforce a 48-hour SLA for ASM approval and a 72-hour SLA for Head Office sign-off. Expired SLAs should auto-escalate and flag for manual intervention. This protects the motivational connection between the sales action and the reward.
Process Config Owner: Alkem operations + Loyalife admin
3
Complete the first program data export and establish a monthly cadence
No transaction data has been extracted since launch. Run the first export to establish a baseline: active members, claims submitted, claims approved/rejected, points issued, and redemptions. Once baseline is established, schedule a monthly export cadence. The account team needs visibility to make informed configuration decisions.
Data Operations Owner: Loyalife CSM + Alkem account manager
4
Add photo evidence requirement for visibility activity claims
Shelf display and promotional placement claims are currently approved without structured evidence. Add a mandatory photo upload field on visibility activity claim forms — one photo per activity type. This reduces manual verification effort for approvers, creates an audit trail, and reduces the risk of duplicate or fraudulent claims. A spot-check review process by the Area Sales Manager at the photo level is faster than full claim-by-claim review.
Claim UX Configuration Owner: Loyalife product + Alkem field ops
5
Confirm KYC requirements and communicate redemption readiness to members
It is unclear whether identity verification is configured as a gate before redemption, and whether members have been informed of what is required to redeem. Before the first redemption cycle opens, confirm the KYC configuration, run a verification campaign among enrolled members, and send a single communication clarifying: how to earn, how to verify, and how to redeem.
Member Enablement Owner: Alkem compliance + Loyalife CSM
